May 2026 · Read Chan · Developer's Message

AI killed my motivation to work on this 8-year-old side project.

After 8 years of solo development, I'm pausing major feature work on Read Chan until at least Q4 2026. I'll still be around to patch anything critical. If you want my reasoning, keep reading.

I built this app for two reasons:

  1. I wanted an app with the features I'm interested in.
  2. I wanted the programmer's high, the kind you only get from coding for hours straight, completely doused in dopamine the entire time.

Reason #1 is basically done. These days, I'm mostly shipping features that other users ask for.

Reason #2 is dead. AI killed it.

For a long time, I didn't use AI on this project. I had the full context of the entire codebase, and I knew that if I started letting AI make changes, that would disappear. I know myself: for personal projects, I'd lose interest in reading through everything the AI wrote. So I held out.

It's ironic because my day job is building AI infra to help engineers at the company I work for use AI more effectively. I spend all day pushing AI agents at work, then come home, refuse to use AI, and hand-write features like it's 2022.

In April, I caved. There was a feature users had long been asking for, and I just didn't have the free time to build it manually. The feature ended up being around 1,000 lines of code, and it took fewer than 20 prompts in a single session. Before AI, that would've taken a few hours a day over several days.

The app went from 59K to 81K lines in a month, although most of those are translation strings for the 20+ supported languages I recently added. I've written over 200K lines over 8 years. Then I watched AI generate 20K in a few sessions.

I tried going back to writing features by hand, but I couldn't do it. The dopamine was gone. My brain just won't release any, because I know I could have the same feature using a few prompts instead of grinding it out manually.

My second reason for working on this app disappeared. After 8 years, the marginal benefit of writing another feature was already close to zero, and AI pushed it into the negative.

So I'm pausing. I need to figure out what comes next for this app.

Thank you for the support over all these years.
